Real-Time People Counting in Confined Spaces

A Project by SEE Telecom

This project tackles a critical challenge in confined environments like tunnels and metro stations: ensuring real-time awareness of occupant numbers.

Enhancing Emergency Response

Imagine an accident within a tunnel or metro station. Knowing the exact number of people inside becomes crucial for first responders. This project aims to provide that crucial information.

By implementing a people-counting system, emergency services can:

  • Deploy resources efficiently based on the number of people potentially affected.
  • Coordinate evacuation efforts more effectively.
  • Improve overall response times during emergencies.

Technology for Safety

This project explores various technologies for accurate people counting, such as:

  • Camera-based systems: Utilizing image recognition or thermal imaging to detect people entering and exiting confined spaces.
  • Sensor-based systems: Employing sensors like LiDAR or infrared to track movement and estimate occupancy.
  • Data integration: Combining data from multiple sources for enhanced accuracy and redundancy.
